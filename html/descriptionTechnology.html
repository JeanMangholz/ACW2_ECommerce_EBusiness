Introduction

Money

Money is a medium of exchange, and we use money as a means of measuring the worth and value of goods and services (University of Minnesota, ND). Before the invention of money, people would trade their wares between each other. Now in the 21st century, physical money is becoming closer and closer to being obsolete (Regalado, 2012). Even though money and its technologies have changed a lot since it was first created, money remains an exchange medium, regardless of what form it takes (Investopedia, 2018).

As time goes by we are moving away from the use of physical money, as money now exists in a purely electronic form. Not only can we conduct money transfers and payment transactions online but now there exists money with little to no physical connection or real-world presence whatsoever. This is called e-money (European Commision, N.D). One example of a type of e-money is currency in video games. Virtual currencies in video games are used to pay for services provided online or acquire virtual goods, and as currency in video games has a fixed exchange rate to real money, that has been defined by its developers, the ‘value’ of this form of virtual money can be decided upon (Vlastov, 2017). Cryptocurrencies are another form of electronic money. A unique form of money due to the fact that it is used solely for online transactions and has absolutely no connections to material objects and only exists as information (Vlastov, 2017).

In this report, the future trends of money and the associated technologies will be discussed, alongside their impacts of the economic market and businesses.
